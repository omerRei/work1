WHO is MISIX?

Misix was born in the USSR, to a Mountain Jewish family in the Caucasus and grew up in Beer sheva, Israel. He began recording music under the name Misix in 1997, sometimes misconceived to be taken from the Asterix comic books. An interview on UK clubbing website Harderfaster.net revealed the name was chosen for simply sounding good, and not directly influenced by anything in particular. Over the last couple of years, studio time was a rare commodity for Misix, but nevertheless he managed to find the extra hours to work on his third studio album for HOMmega. "Red Means Distortion" is the result of some very late night sessions.[citation needed] The "Acid Rocker" EP, which was released in August 2010, was the first release new studio album, "Red Means Distortion". As bonus material, it included a remix by trance artist Pixel of "Closer To Heaven" featured vocalist Michele Adamson, and a fresh new GMS 2010 remix to Misix's "Eye To Eye". In September, a month after the EP "Acid rocker" was released, the third album "Red means distortion" was released. Starting in August 2010, Misix has been creating mix sessions titled "Trance for Nations"
